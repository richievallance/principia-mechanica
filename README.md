### Core Argument

The Principia argues that Leonardo da Vinci's surviving corpus is governed by a **universal architecture of motion, medium, form and memory**. The four classical elements are not decorative symbols; they are operative media through which the universal law manifests:

- **Earth** — the field of resistance
- **Air** — the field of transmission  
- **Fire** — the field of transformation
- **Water** — the field of flow and return
- **The Fifth Element** — the ordered relation by which the four become one working reality

The root law, confirmed in six instances across five stages of Madrid Codex II:

> *La cosa che ssi move acquissta tanto di spatio quanto ella ne lasscia.*  
> 'The thing that moves acquires as much space as it leaves.' — f.116r

### Madrid Codex II as Proof-Case

The main body of Madrid Codex II (ff.16r–140v) divides into eight working stages of 16 folios each. The Principia documents:

- Three hard dates confirmed from BNE images (20 Nov 1504, 30 Nov 1504, 6 June 1505)
- *Della memoria lochale* in Leonardo's locked-chest library (f.4v)
- The Conceptione formally named and used as proof authority in the Horse Fascicle
- Three Facetie as memory anchors encoding the conservation law
- The viola organista specification as fifth-element instrument
- The cancelled epitaph on f.144r confirming grief at the lost Sforza Horse
